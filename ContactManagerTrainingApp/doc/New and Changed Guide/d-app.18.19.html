Splitting and Spinning Policies

In PolicyCenter 7.0, you can now split off two policies from an existing policy. In addition, you can spin off a single policy from an existing policy.
In the default configuration, this functionality is available in the personal auto line of business. By configuring PolicyCenter, you can add this functionality to other lines of business.
In personal auto, you might want to split or spin a policy for one of the following reasons:
•
Split – A couple gets divorced. Both spouses wish to remain with the carrier. The carrier creates two new accounts, and splits the coverables on the existing policy into coverables on policies in the new accounts. The split creates two submission jobs.
•
Spin – A son moves out of the house, and takes a car that is on his parents’ policy. The carrier creates a new account for the son, and moves the car from the parents’ policy onto a new policy on the son’s account. Spin creates a single submission.
